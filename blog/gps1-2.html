What is pair programming?

Pair programming is when two or more programmers work in unison to create a finished product. Contrary to the popular lone programmer stereotype, pair programming is actually increasingly seen in the workplace as well.

Why is pair programming important to DBC?

Pair programming is important to DBC because they want to prepare us for real-world situations, which commonly include peer programming. It would also prepare students for remote work situations as well as foster empathy engineering best practices.

Compare Driver and Navigator

In peer programming the driver is doing most of the typing/coding while the navigator fascilitates the flow of logic.
